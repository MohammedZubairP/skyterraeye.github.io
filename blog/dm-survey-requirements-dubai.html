Dubai Municipality (DM) has specific technical requirements for all land survey drawings submitted for building permits, completion certificates, plot registration and other municipal approvals. Getting these wrong is one of the most common and expensive mistakes made by developers and contractors in Dubai. This guide explains every DM survey requirement clearly.

The Three Core DM Survey Requirements

Every survey submitted to Dubai Municipality must use three specific technical standards:

1. WGS84 — Horizontal Datum

WGS84 (World Geodetic System 1984) is the global coordinate system used by GPS. All survey coordinates in Dubai must be referenced to WGS84. This means your surveyor must connect their work to the UAE national GNSS network — not use a local, arbitrary site grid.

2. UTM Zone 40N — Map Projection

UTM Zone 40N (Universal Transverse Mercator) converts WGS84 latitude/longitude into flat Easting/Northing grid coordinates used on engineering drawings. Dubai falls in UTM Zone 40N. Do not use a local site grid, UTM Zone 39N, or any other projection.

3. EGM2008 — Vertical Datum (Height System)

EGM2008 (Earth Gravitational Model 2008) converts GPS ellipsoidal heights into orthometric heights (what a spirit level measures — the height water would actually flow to). DM requires all reduced levels to be in EGM2008 orthometric heights. This is the most commonly missed requirement — GPS readings alone are NOT in EGM2008 and must be converted.

DM Drawing Requirements

In addition to the correct coordinate system, DM survey drawings must include:

  • Layer naming — specific DM Spatial Data layer names (not AutoCAD default layers)
  • Title block — project number, date, scale, north point, coordinate system statement
  • Surveyor certification — licensed surveyor stamp and signature on every sheet
  • Control point information — reference to DM-registered primary control points used
  • Scale bar — graphical scale bar plus stated scale (e.g. 1:500)
  • Deviation table — design vs actual for as-built surveys

Common DM Submission Rejection Reasons

  1. Wrong coordinate system — local grid instead of WGS84/UTM Zone 40N/EGM2008
  2. GPS heights not converted to EGM2008 orthometric heights
  3. Survey not tied to DM-registered control points
  4. Incorrect DWG layer naming
  5. Missing surveyor certification stamp
  6. Deviation table missing from as-built drawings
  7. Floor levels not at required density

Frequently Asked Questions

What coordinate system does Dubai Municipality require?
+
Dubai Municipality requires all survey drawings to use WGS84 horizontal datum, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 height datum. Using any other coordinate system will result in DM rejection.
What is EGM2008 in Dubai surveying?
+
EGM2008 (Earth Gravitational Model 2008) is the geoid model used to convert GPS ellipsoidal heights to orthometric heights (the heights water flows to and that spirit levels measure). Dubai Municipality requires all reduced levels in EGM2008. GPS heights without geoid correction are NOT acceptable to DM.
Why do surveys get rejected by Dubai Municipality?
+
The most common DM rejection reasons are: wrong coordinate system, GPS heights not converted to EGM2008, survey not tied to DM control points, incorrect AutoCAD layer naming, missing surveyor stamp and missing deviation tables on as-built drawings.
What accuracy class does DM require for building surveys?
+
For construction setting out and as-built surveys, Dubai Municipality requires minimum Class C accuracy (±25mm horizontal, ±20mm vertical). Sky TerraEye uses Leica and Trimble instruments that achieve Class B or better accuracy on all projects.
