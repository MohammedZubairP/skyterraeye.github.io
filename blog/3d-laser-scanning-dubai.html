3D laser scanning in Dubai is the process of capturing millions of precise 3D measurements of a building, structure or site using a terrestrial laser scanner. The result is a dense 3D point cloud that can be used for as-built documentation, BIM modelling, clash detection, structural analysis, renovation design and facility management across construction and industrial projects in the UAE.

What is a 3D Laser Scanner?

A terrestrial laser scanner emits rapid laser pulses in all directions, measuring the distance to every surface it can see. A typical high-specification scanner used by Sky TerraEye captures up to 2 million points per second with an accuracy of ±1-2mm at ranges of up to 270 metres. Multiple scanner positions are combined (registered) to create a seamless 3D point cloud of the entire building or site.

3D Laser Scanning Applications in Dubai

  • As-built documentation — precise 3D documentation of existing and completed buildings for records and DM submission
  • BIM model creation — scan-to-BIM for Revit, ArchiCAD and other BIM platforms at any Level of Detail
  • Renovation and fit-out — accurate existing condition surveys for interior redesign and M&E coordination
  • Structural inspection — facades, columns, beams and slabs measured for deflection, deformation and distress
  • Industrial plant documentation — oil and gas, processing plants and utility facilities captured for asset management
  • Heritage preservation — monuments and heritage buildings documented for restoration
  • Volume calculation — stockpile, earthworks and cavity volumes from point cloud data

Scan-to-BIM in Dubai

Scan-to-BIM is the process of creating a Revit, ArchiCAD or IFC BIM model directly from 3D scan point cloud data. Sky TerraEye delivers scan-to-BIM models at any Level of Detail (LOD 100 to LOD 500) as required by your project team, including:

  • Structural elements — columns, beams, slabs, walls and cores
  • Architectural features — facades, ceilings, doors, windows and stairs
  • MEP services — visible pipework, ductwork and cable trays
  • Site context — existing buildings, roads and ground levels around the project
