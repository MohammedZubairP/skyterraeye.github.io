Dubai Municipality Survey Standards 2026

Dubai Municipality (DM) requires all survey drawings to comply with DM Spatial Data Standards using WGS84 horizontal datum, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 orthometric heights. Survey drawings must use correct DM layer naming, include a surveyor’s certification stamp and reference DM-registered primary control monuments.

When Land Surveys are Required in UAE 2026

  • Building permit applications — topographic and boundary survey required before DM approves permits
  • Construction phase — setting out sign-off at foundation, structural frame and completion
  • Building completion certificate — as-built survey mandatory for DM BCC issuance
  • DLD land registration — boundary survey matching DLD registered coordinates
  • Infrastructure handover — road, utility and drainage as-built surveys before authority adoption

EGM2008 — Why It Matters in Dubai

GPS receivers measure ellipsoidal heights — heights above the mathematical shape of the Earth. Dubai Municipality requires orthometric heights — the heights water would actually flow to. The difference in Dubai is approximately +22 to +25 metres. Without applying the EGM2008 geoid model, all your survey levels will be 22–25m wrong. Frequently Asked Questions

What coordinate system is used for land surveys in UAE?
+
Dubai Municipality requires WGS84 datum, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 height datum. Abu Dhabi DMT uses WGS84/UTM Zone 40N with the Abu Dhabi Local Height Datum instead of EGM2008.
When is an as-built survey required in Dubai?
+
As-built surveys are mandatory for Dubai Municipality Building Completion Certificates. They must be submitted in WGS84/UTM Zone 40N/EGM2008 with correct DM layer naming before DM issues the completion certificate.
What is Class B survey accuracy in Dubai?
+
Class B survey accuracy in Dubai is ±10mm horizontal and ±10mm vertical. This is required for GNSS control networks and construction control surveys. Sky TerraEye uses Leica and Trimble instruments achieving Class B accuracy.
