Getting a building permit in Dubai requires specific survey drawings that Dubai Municipality uses to verify your plot boundaries, existing site conditions and proposed building position. Missing or incorrect surveys are one of the most common reasons for building permit delays in Dubai.

Surveys Required for a DM Building Permit

  • Topographic Survey — existing ground levels, contours and features of your plot in WGS84/UTM Zone 40N/EGM2008. Required for DM site plan submission.
  • Boundary Survey / Plot Plan — confirmed plot boundaries from DLD coordinates, with area calculation matching title deed.
  • Existing Features Survey — any existing structures, trees, walls or services on the plot that must be shown on DM drawings.
  • Road Level Survey — finished road levels at your plot frontage to determine minimum floor level requirements.

The DM Building Permit Survey Process

  1. Your architect requests a topographic survey from Sky TerraEye with your DLD title deed plot number
  2. We survey the plot, including boundary coordinates, ground levels and existing features
  3. We deliver AutoCAD DWG in WGS84/UTM Zone 40N/EGM2008 within 24 hours
  4. Your architect uses this as the base drawing for the building design
  5. The drawings are submitted to DM via Ejari or direct DM portal
  6. DM checks the survey is in the correct coordinate system and signed by a licensed surveyor
