What is an As-Built Survey — in Plain English

Imagine your approved design shows Column A1 at coordinate X=12,345.00, Y=56,789.00. After construction, the column might actually be at X=12,344.85 — 15mm off. An as-built survey measures where everything actually ended up and produces a drawing showing design vs actual positions with any deviations highlighted.

This drawing goes to Dubai Municipality as proof your building was built within acceptable tolerances — mandatory before they issue your Building Completion Certificate (BCC) or Occupation Permit.

🎓 What the Deviation Table in Your Drawing Means

Your as-built drawing will include a deviation table listing every structural element with its design coordinate, actual coordinate and the difference (deviation). A deviation of less than 25mm is generally accepted by DM without comment. Between 25–50mm, DM may ask for a structural engineer’s comment. Above 50mm, you may need to demonstrate no structural impact. Sky TerraEye flags any large deviations in our reports so you know before we submit.

When You Need an As-Built Survey in Dubai

  • After foundation completion — verify pile/column positions before the next slab pour

What DM Checks in Your As-Built Drawing

After seeing dozens of rejected drawings from other surveyors, here is the complete DM checklist:

  • Coordinate system: WGS84 datum, UTM Zone 40N projection, EGM2008 heights — all three required
  • Control points: Survey must reference DM-registered primary control points
  • Deviation table: Design vs actual for all structural elements — not just the actual coordinates
  • Floor levels: Finished floor levels (FFL) at minimum every 10m across each slab
  • DWG layer naming: Must follow DM Spatial Data layer standards — not AutoCAD defaults
  • Surveyor stamp: Licensed surveyor’s certification stamp on every drawing sheet
  • Title block: Project number, date, scale, north point, coordinate system note

5 Common Reasons DM Rejects As-Built Surveys

  1. Wrong coordinate system — using local site grid instead of WGS84/UTM Zone 40N. Most common rejection reason.
  2. No EGM2008 heights — GPS heights must be converted to orthometric heights using EGM2008. Many surveyors skip this step.
  3. Missing deviation table — DM wants to see design vs actual. Actual coordinates alone is not enough.
  4. Wrong DWG layer naming — DM has specific layer requirements. Default AutoCAD layers cause rejection.
  5. No independent control check — survey not tied to registered DM control points.
