GNSS / GPS RTK Survey in Dubai — Complete Guide (2025)

GNSS RTK Survey Dubai

A GNSS survey in Dubai (Global Navigation Satellite System) uses signals from GPS, GLONASS, Galileo and BeiDou satellites to determine precise three-dimensional coordinates on the ground. GNSS RTK (Real-Time Kinematic) surveys are widely used across Dubai and the UAE for construction control, topographic mapping, boundary demarcation and utility as-built surveys.

What is GNSS RTK Surveying?

RTK (Real-Time Kinematic) GNSS surveying uses a base station or network of continuously operating reference stations (CORS) to correct satellite signals in real time, delivering centimetre-level accuracy at the rover (field receiver). In Dubai, Sky TerraEye uses the UAE National CORS Network (Network RTK) and Leica and Trimble GNSS receivers for all projects.

The main advantage of GNSS RTK over total station surveying is speed — a GNSS rover can survey hundreds of points per hour over large open areas without line-of-sight limitations. However, total stations are still required in areas with poor satellite visibility such as underground spaces, building interiors and dense urban canyons.

GNSS Survey Applications in Dubai

  • Construction Control Networks — Establishing primary and secondary control points for large construction sites, tied to DM datum
  • Topographic Surveys — Rapid mapping of open sites, roads, utilities and terrain using GNSS rovers
  • Boundary Surveys — Locating and confirming land boundaries for DLD registration and boundary disputes
  • As-Built Surveys — Post-construction verification of road alignments, utility positions and structural elements
  • Earthworks Monitoring — Rapid cut/fill monitoring and volume surveys on large earthworks sites
  • Coordinate Transformation — Converting coordinates between local grid systems and the DM national coordinate system

For Dubai Municipality submissions, all GNSS control points must achieve Class A (±5mm) or Class B (±10mm) accuracy depending on the project type. Sky TerraEye always specifies the appropriate observation duration to meet the required accuracy class.

DM Coordinate System for GNSS Surveys in Dubai

All GNSS surveys in Dubai must be delivered in the correct datum and projection:

  • Horizontal Datum: WGS84 (ITRF2014)
  • Projection: UTM Zone 40N
  • Vertical Datum: EGM2008 geoid model (orthometric heights)
  • Epoch: As specified by DM for the project date

Important: GPS-only GNSS receivers cannot provide EGM2008 ellipsoidal-to-orthometric height conversion. Sky TerraEye uses receivers with the correct geoid model applied, ensuring heights are accepted by DM on first submission.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a GNSS RTK survey in Dubai?+

A GNSS RTK survey uses satellite signals corrected by a network of reference stations to determine precise coordinates in real time. In Dubai, it is used for construction control, topographic mapping, boundary surveys and as-built documentation.

What accuracy does GNSS RTK achieve in Dubai?+

Network RTK achieves ±10mm horizontal and ±20mm vertical accuracy in Dubai. For higher accuracy, static GNSS observations over 1-4 hours can achieve ±1-3mm horizontal. Sky TerraEye selects the method based on your project's DM accuracy class requirements.

How much does a GNSS survey cost in Dubai?+

GNSS surveys in Dubai start from AED 1,800 for a half-day Network RTK survey. Control network establishment and static GNSS processing are priced based on number of points and required accuracy. Contact Sky TerraEye for a free quote.

Do GNSS surveys need DM approval in Dubai?+

GNSS control networks and boundary surveys must comply with Dubai Municipality Spatial Data standards — WGS84 datum, UTM Zone 40N projection and EGM2008 heights. Sky TerraEye delivers all GNSS surveys in the correct DM format with a 99% first-time approval rate.

What is the difference between GPS and GNSS surveys?+

GPS uses only US satellites. GNSS uses all satellite constellations — GPS, GLONASS (Russia), Galileo (Europe) and BeiDou (China) — giving more satellites in view, better accuracy and more reliable positioning in difficult environments like urban Dubai.
