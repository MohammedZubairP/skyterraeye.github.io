Total Station Survey in Dubai — Complete Guide (2025)

Total Station Survey Dubai

A total station survey in Dubai is one of the most common and essential surveying methods used on construction sites, infrastructure projects and land development across the UAE. This guide explains what a total station survey is, how it works, what it costs in Dubai, and how to choose the best surveying company for your project.

What is a Total Station Survey?

A total station is an electronic surveying instrument that combines an optical theodolite (for measuring angles) with an electronic distance meter (EDM) to calculate precise three-dimensional coordinates of points in the field. In Dubai, total station surveys are used for almost every type of construction and land survey work.

The instrument measures horizontal angles, vertical angles and slope distances simultaneously, then computes X, Y and Z coordinates automatically. Modern total stations like the Leica TS16 and Trimble S9 used by Sky TerraEye can achieve angular accuracy of 1” (one arcsecond) and distance accuracy of ±1mm + 1.5ppm.

Types of Total Station Surveys in Dubai

Sky TerraEye provides all types of total station survey services across Dubai and the UAE:

  • Setting Out Survey (Layout Survey) — Marking the positions of columns, walls, grids and foundations on site from approved design drawings. This is the most requested total station survey type in Dubai.
  • As-Built Survey — Verifying and documenting the actual as-constructed positions of structural elements against design. Required for Dubai Municipality building completion certificates.
  • Topographic Survey — Measuring the natural and man-made features of a site including contours, spot levels and planimetric detail for design purposes.
  • Control Survey — Establishing a network of precise reference points (control points) for a construction site, tied to the DM national coordinate system.
  • Traverse Survey — Running a series of connected lines to extend control across a large site or between disconnected areas.
  • Boundary Survey — Measuring and marking the legal boundaries of a land parcel for registration with the Dubai Land Department (DLD).

Dubai Municipality (DM) Compliance

All total station surveys in Dubai must be delivered in the correct coordinate system as required by Dubai Municipality Spatial Data standards:

  • Horizontal Datum: WGS84 (World Geodetic System 1984)
  • Projection: UTM Zone 40N
  • Vertical Datum: EGM2008 (Earth Gravitational Model 2008)

Sky TerraEye delivers all total station surveys in this datum combination and has a 99% first-time DM submission pass rate. Our AutoCAD DWG files are formatted to DM standards and include all required layers, annotations and title block information.
