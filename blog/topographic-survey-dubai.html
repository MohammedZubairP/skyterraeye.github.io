What is a Topographic Survey?

A topographic survey — sometimes called a topo survey, terrain survey or site survey — uses a total station and/or GNSS RTK rover to measure thousands of points across a site at regular intervals. These points are then processed to generate contour lines, spot heights, a digital terrain model (DTM) and a full planimetric drawing of everything on and adjacent to the site.

In Dubai, topographic surveys are used by architects and civil engineers as the base drawing for design, by contractors for earthworks planning and by authorities for masterplan and infrastructure approvals.

What Does a Topographic Survey Include?

  • Spot heights and contours — ground levels at regular grid intervals, typically 0.5m or 1m contours
  • Existing structures — buildings, boundary walls, fences and hard surfaces
  • Roads and kerbs — road edges, kerb lines, footpaths and carriageway levels
  • Trees and vegetation — positions and trunk sizes of significant trees
  • Visible utilities — manhole covers, valve boxes, cable markers and street furniture
  • Boundary information — plot boundaries, fencing and gates
  • Digital Terrain Model (DTM) — 3D surface model of the existing ground for earthworks calculation

When Do You Need a Topographic Survey in Dubai?

  • Before submitting a planning application to Dubai Municipality
  • Before appointing a structural or civil engineer for design
  • For earthworks volume calculations and cut/fill design
  • For drainage and road design
  • Before purchasing land — to verify levels, boundaries and existing features
  • For masterplan development and urban planning submissions
  • Before drone surveys — topographic ground control points are required
