What is a Topographic Survey — Explained Simply

A topo survey is like a 3D photograph of your site converted into a drawing. Instead of just showing the outline of the land, it shows the height of every point across the site by measuring thousands of ground points with a total station or GNSS rover.

The result is a drawing with:

  • Contour lines — lines joining equal-height points (like the rings on a hill). In Dubai we produce 0.25m or 0.5m contours.
  • Spot heights — individual level readings at key points like building corners and drain covers
  • Site features — existing buildings, walls, trees, roads, kerbs, utilities, boundaries
  • DTM — a 3D digital surface model for earthworks calculations and drainage design
🎓 How to Read a Topographic Survey Drawing

Contour lines close together = steep slope. Contour lines far apart = gentle slope. The number on each contour line is the reduced level (height above mean sea level) in EGM2008 datum. In Dubai, most ground levels fall between +1.0m and +10.0m RL. Your road level at the site boundary is the critical number — your engineer uses this to set your finished floor level (usually +0.15m to +0.30m above road level minimum).

When You Need a Topographic Survey in Dubai

  • Before appointing an architect — they cannot start without knowing the levels
  • Before DM planning submission — DM requires a topographic base for site plans
  • Before earthworks tendering — calculate exact cut/fill volumes before pricing
  • Before drainage design — understand natural fall direction and catchment
  • Before buying land — confirm levels, features and any encroachments
  • For masterplan development — large-scale topo for whole communities

💡 Pro Tip: What to Tell Your Surveyor at Booking

Always ask your surveyor to include visible utility manholes, valve boxes and streetlights in the topographic survey — it costs nothing extra but saves your M&E engineer multiple site visits. Also confirm you want the road levels at your site boundary included — this determines your minimum finished floor level for DM compliance.

What Your Architect Actually Needs from the Topo

We work with architects and civil engineers in Dubai every day. Here is what they always ask for — and what we include as standard:

  • DWG in WGS84/UTM Zone 40N/EGM2008 — ready to use directly, correct for DM
  • Contours at 0.25m or 0.5m intervals
  • Spot heights at corners, breaks in gradient and key site features
  • All existing structures with external dimensions
  • Trees with trunk diameter (needed for DM landscape submissions)
  • Road levels at site frontage — critical for designing your gate and finished floor level
  • Plot boundary from DLD title deed coordinates plotted onto the drawing
